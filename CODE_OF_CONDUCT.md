# Code of Conduct

Modula is built in the open and welcomes contribution from anyone.
A healthy, productive community is the prerequisite for that.

## Our standard

We adopt the [Contributor Covenant, v2.1][covenant] as the formal code
of conduct for this project. By participating in any Modula space —
this repository's issues and pull requests, the Modula social
accounts, the Modula Discord, or any in-person Modula event — you
agree to follow it.

The Covenant in one sentence: **be respectful, assume good faith, and
keep the focus on the work.**

## Scope

This code applies to all project spaces, and also when an individual is
representing the project or its community in public. Representation
includes using an official email, posting from an official social
account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it
to the project maintainers at **conduct@modulabase.org**. All reports
are reviewed promptly, kept confidential, and handled in line with the
Covenant's enforcement guidelines.

Maintainers who do not uphold this code in good faith may face
temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members
of the project leadership.

## Why a code of conduct

Open source is a collaboration with strangers. Clear ground rules let
us spend our energy on the protocol, not on relitigating norms. The
Covenant is the most widely adopted, well-tested standard available,
and we are happy to stand on its shoulders.

[covenant]: https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/
